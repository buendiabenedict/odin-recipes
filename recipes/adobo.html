Adobo

Description

Philippine adobo is a popular Filipino dish and cooking process in Philippine cuisine. In its base form, meat, seafood, or vegetables are first browned in oil, and then marinated and simmered in vinegar, salt and/or soy sauce, and garlic. It is often considered the unofficial national dish in the Philippines.

Ingredients

  • Manok o Baboy
  • Toyo
  • Suka
  • Bawang
  • Pamintang Buo
  • Dahon ng laurel
  • Mantika
  • Tubig
  • Asukal

Steps

  1. I-marinate: Karne, toyo, at bawang.
  2. Igisa: Karne hanggang mag-brown.
  3. Pakuluan: Marinade, tubig, paminta, at laurel hanggang lumambot.
  4. Asim: Ibuhos ang suka, huwag haluin hanggang kumulo.
  5. Piga: Lutuin hanggang lumapot o mag-mantika.
